I am currently a Sofware Engineer in the Boston Area. I graduated with a Master's Degree in Robotics at Northeastern University in Boston, MA. While I was working on my degree, I worked in the NEU Field Robotics Lab on using thermal cameras to generate visual odometry in environments where traditional cameras would fail.

I received two Honors Bachelors Degrees in Computer Engineering and Electrical Engineering from Southern Illinois University Carbondale. While studying at SIUC, I wrote my Honors Thesis on Approximate Computing with Course Grained Reconfigurable Architecture.

I'm passionate about all things robotic, and I've worked on many personal projects involving robotics. One of my personal favorites is my work on the Robot "Winston", who went on to win first place overall at the 2017 ATMAE National Robotics Competition.
